Tips for Thriving in College

From a second year engineering student at UW-Madison

Get Started

Academic Tips

Find a community

Creating relationships with your classmates, TA’s, and professors will make keeping up with your school work more enjoyable. You can do this by going to office hours, talking to the people you sit next to in class, and joining more clubs you are interested in.

Stay organized

It is easy to get overwhelmed in college and using a planner to keep track of your daily tasks can help you lower your stress. This will help you focus on your top priority tasks.

Ask for help

Take advantage of your university's help services. Whether it is free tutoring, career services, or health services, use it to better yourself.

Personal Anecdote

In my first semester of college I struggled to transition to the rigorous academic responsibilities. I was often overwhelmed and it showed in my first midterm grades. I started getting tutoring, asking my professors questions at the end of lectures, and prioritizing my mental health. After finding these strategies, I was able to improve my grades by the end of the semester. I have since then included these practices into my daily schedule to ensure a balanced and successful academic experience.

Move your body

The best way to give your brain the refresh it needs after a long day of classes is to move your body.

Get your sleep

When you sleep, your brain is processing the information it is given throughout the day. This is why it is so important we give it enough time (7 to 10 hours) to rest every night.
